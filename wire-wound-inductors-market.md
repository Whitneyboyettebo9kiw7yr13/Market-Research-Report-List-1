<p><p>Wire wound inductors are divided into two main types: shielded and non-shielded. </p><p>Shielded wire wound inductors are designed with a magnetic shield to prevent interference with surrounding components, making them ideal for use in high-density electronic applications where electromagnetic interference is a concern. </p><p>Non-shielded wire wound inductors do not have this magnetic shield, making them more cost-effective and suitable for applications where interference is not a major issue. Both types offer different advantages depending on the specific needs of the electronic circuit.</p></p>
