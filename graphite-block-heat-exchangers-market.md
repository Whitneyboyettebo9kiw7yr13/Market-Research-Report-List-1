<p><p>Graphite block heat exchangers come in two main types - Standard and Fine Grain. Standard graphite blocks are typically made with larger graphite particles and have a coarser structure, making them more economical but less efficient. Fine grain graphite blocks, on the other hand, are manufactured with smaller graphite particles, resulting in a denser and more uniform structure that provides higher heat transfer efficiency. Fine grain blocks are usually used in applications requiring higher performance and precision.</p></p>
