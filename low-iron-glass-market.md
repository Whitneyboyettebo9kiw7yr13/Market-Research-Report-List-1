<p><p>Low iron glass is a type of glass with lower iron content, resulting in a higher transparency and clearer appearance compared to regular glass. Float glass is a manufacturing process where molten glass is poured onto a bed of molten metal, creating a flat and even surface. Rolled glass, on the other hand, is produced by passing molten glass through a set of rollers, creating a textured or patterned surface. Both float glass and rolled glass can be manufactured as low iron glass, offering enhanced clarity and aesthetics for various applications like windows, doors, and architectural designs.</p></p>
