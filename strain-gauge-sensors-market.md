<p><p>Strain gauge sensors are commonly divided into two main types: metal strain gauge sensors and semiconductor strain gauge sensors. Metal strain gauge sensors are made of metallic materials like steel or copper, and their resistance changes with mechanical strain. Semiconductor strain gauge sensors use semiconductors like silicon to detect strain and convert it into an electrical signal. Both types of sensors are widely used in applications like structural health monitoring, industrial automation, and automotive testing due to their high sensitivity and reliability in measuring strain.</p></p>
