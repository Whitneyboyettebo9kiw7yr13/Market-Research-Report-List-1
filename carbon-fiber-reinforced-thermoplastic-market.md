<p><p>Carbon Fiber Reinforced Thermoplastic (CFRTP) materials are commonly classified into two main types: Polyacrylonitrile (PAN)-based CFRTP and Pitch-based CFRTP. PAN-based CFRTP is derived from polyacrylonitrile fibers, which are processed to create high-strength carbon fibers. On the other hand, Pitch-based CFRTP is produced from carbonaceous pitch, which is a byproduct of the petroleum refining process. Both types offer lightweight and high-strength characteristics, making them popular in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, and sports equipment manufacturing.</p></p>
