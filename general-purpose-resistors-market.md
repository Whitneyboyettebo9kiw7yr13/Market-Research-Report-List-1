<p><p>General Purpose Resistors are widely used in electronic circuits for limiting current or voltage. The market is divided into two main types: SMD Type and Through Hole Type. SMD Type resistors are surface mount devices that are soldered directly onto the circuit board, making them ideal for compact electronic designs. Through Hole Type resistors, on the other hand, have leads that are inserted through holes in the circuit board and soldered in place. Both types are commonly used in various electronic applications due to their versatility and reliability.</p></p>
