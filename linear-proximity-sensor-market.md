<p><strong>The Linear Proximity Sensor Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Resistive</li><li>Magnetoresistive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Linear proximity sensors are commonly classified into resistive and magnetoresistive types based on their underlying technologies. Resistive sensors operate by measuring the change in resistance caused by the displacement of a wiper along a resistive element, providing precise linear measurements in applications such as industrial automation and robotics. On the other hand, magnetoresistive sensors utilize the change in magnetic field strength to determine the position of a target, offering high sensitivity and robustness in harsh environments like automotive and aerospace industries.</p></p>
