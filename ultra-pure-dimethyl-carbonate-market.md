<p><p>Ultra pure dimethyl carbonate is commonly produced using two methods: Propylene Oxide Method and Ethylene Oxide Method. The Propylene Oxide Method involves the reaction of propylene oxide with methanol to produce dimethyl carbonate, while the Ethylene Oxide Method involves the reaction of ethylene oxide with methanol. Both methods result in high purity dimethyl carbonate suitable for a variety of applications such as electrolytes in lithium ion batteries, polycarbonates, and pesticides. These markets cater to industries requiring high quality and performance.</p></p>
