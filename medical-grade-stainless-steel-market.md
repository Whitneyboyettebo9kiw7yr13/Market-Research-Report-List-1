<p><p>Medical grade stainless steel comes in various types, with the most common being 304 and 316. Type 304 is known for its high corrosion resistance and durability, making it ideal for medical equipment and devices. Type 316 offers even greater resistance to corrosion and is often used in implants and surgical instruments. Other types of medical grade stainless steel include 316L and 316Ti, each offering specific benefits for different medical applications. Overall, these types of stainless steel are crucial in maintaining hygiene and safety in healthcare settings.</p></p>
