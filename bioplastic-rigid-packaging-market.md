<p><p>Bioplastic rigid packaging is classified into two main types: Bio-PET and Bio-PP. Bio-PET, or bio-based polyethylene terephthalate, is a type of bioplastic made from renewable sources such as sugarcane or corn. It is commonly used in bottling applications due to its strength and durability. Bio-PP, or bio-based polypropylene, is another type of bioplastic derived from plant-based sources. It is known for its versatility and is used in various rigid packaging applications, including containers and caps. Both Bio-PET and Bio-PP are sustainable alternatives to traditional plastics, reducing reliance on fossil fuels and lowering carbon emissions.</p></p>
