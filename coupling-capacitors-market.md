High voltage coupling capacitors are designed for high power applications where voltage levels are above a certain threshold, usually ranging from hundreds to thousands of volts. These capacitors are used in transmission lines, power supplies, and electric vehicles. On the other hand, low voltage coupling capacitors are suitable for applications with lower voltage requirements, typically below a hundred volts, such as audio equipment, medical devices, and consumer electronics.</p></p>
